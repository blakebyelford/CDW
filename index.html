CASE / 01 01

"A Stay That Moves You"

An integrated campaign for the U.S. Hospitality segment at Life Fitness.

The Challenge

The U.S. Hospitality segment had never run a full-funnel integrated campaign. Messaging was product-centric. Channels operated in silos. Field sellers were asking for tools they didn't have, and the segment was leaving demand on the table in a market where Life Fitness held an 18.3% NAMER share.

The Approach

I built the campaign concept and brief end-to-end: four messaging pillars, a positioning shift from product-centric to operator-centric, and an orchestration plan that tied social, content, SEO, web, email, and trade media into a single narrative. Then I worked across functions to ship it without an external agency.

The Execution

Campaign launched across six channels in coordinated waves. The Hospitality Guide and segment landing pages were rebuilt from scratch, mapped to a six-touch email sequence, and amplified through trade journal placements and event activations. Sales got a refreshed enablement library tied to the campaign messaging.

AI in the workflow

Custom agents replaced the missing agency.

The segment didn't have agency budget. Instead of outsourcing the missing roles, I built a small system of Claude-powered agents modeled after a creative shop: a strategist, a copywriter, a creative director, and a planner.

Each agent had its own brief, its own voice, and a task scope. I'd run drafts through them in sequence, then take an 80/20 cut on whatever came back to refine into the final asset. The result wasn't AI doing the work, it was AI compressing the loop between strategy and execution.

CASE / 02 02

ABM in action: warranty + CapEx

Two account-based plays, one CRM stack, $1M+ in attributed pipeline.

The Challenge

Multi-Unit Housing had a known pool of warranty-eligible accounts and a hospitality vertical that was missing its annual CapEx planning window entirely. Both were wide-open opportunities, and both required precision: the right accounts, the right reps, the right window.

The Approach

I built two ABM plays around the same engine. For warranty: a Pardot Engagement Studio sequence sent from individual sellers' inboxes, sourced from Salesforce + ZoomInfo enrichment. For CapEx: a seasonality-based two-touch sequence to ~18K hospitality contacts, with Microsoft Bookings introduced as a low-friction CTA so reps could schedule consultations without back-and-forth.

The Execution

Both campaigns ran out of the same Pardot infrastructure I'd previously rebuilt for funnel-stage tracking. Each touchpoint was scored, each opportunity attributed back through Salesforce. The CapEx campaign was the segment's first seasonality-based program; we onboarded every key account manager onto Bookings before launch.

Personalization, scaled by hand.

CASE / 03 03

The paid search turnaround

A $50K/month media spend, gone wrong, then put right at Fuel Me.

The Challenge

Fuel Me's paid search was running at roughly $900 per lead. Lead quality was inconsistent, the funnel wasn't converting, and the spend was a real percentage of monthly burn. As sole marketer, I owned the diagnosis and the fix.

The Approach

I started with intent. Audited every active keyword group against the actual buyer search behavior we were seeing in HubSpot, then pulled SQL data from sales to map closed-won accounts back to their original entry queries. The pattern was clear: we were buying volume, not intent. The keyword strategy had drifted from the ICP.

The Execution

Restructured the account around tighter intent clusters, built negative keyword lists from the bottom up, rewrote ad copy to match buyer language, and overhauled the landing pages to mirror the search query. Then ran the optimization through an offshore execution team on a weekly cadence.

The diagnostic mindset

Performance work is hypothesis work.

This one predates my heaviest AI use, but the discipline is the same one I now apply with Claude in the loop. Frame the hypothesis. Pull the data. Test the cheapest variant first. Don't ship the next round until you can name what you learned from the last one.

It's the same approach CDW's JD describes when it asks for "sharp analytical instincts" and continuous improvement against MQLs, conversion rates, and ROI.

Methodology

How I actually use AI in campaign work.

Not "uses ChatGPT for emails." A real workflow that treats AI as a junior team you direct, review, and edit. Below: the four agents I most often run, and the philosophy underneath them.

Agent / 01

The Strategist

Pressure-tests positioning. Given a brief, returns the three competing angles before I commit. Stress-tests the one I pick against likely objections.

Agent / 02

The Copywriter

Drafts variants in the voice of the brand or the seller, depending on the channel. Calibrated against tone samples I provide upfront, not against generic "marketing copy."

Agent / 03

The Creative Director

Critiques the work the way a CD would in a review: what's the idea, where does it land, where does it strain. Turns 80% drafts into 95% drafts faster.

Agent / 04

The Channel Planner

Maps a campaign concept to channel logic and event strategy by funnel stage. Outputs a structured plan I can take to a cross-functional working session.

AI fluency isn't about replacing the marketer. It's about getting to the 80% draft fast enough that the human work, the editing, the judgment, the strategy, can actually breathe. That's what I bring to a campaign team.
— Blake Elford, philosophy on AI in marketing work
